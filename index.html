From Superficial Outputs to Superficial Learning: Risks of Large Language Models in Education

Iris Delikoura, Yi.R Fung, Pan Hui

A systematic review of 70 empirical studies across computer science, education, and psychology examining the risks of LLMs in education. We propose an LLM-Risk Adapted Learning Model that illustrates how technical risks cascade through interaction and interpretation to shape educational outcomes.

arXiv preprint, 2025

Read Paper

Featured in Forbes

Our paper "From Superficial Outputs to Superficial Learning" was selected as one of the 8 AI Research Papers Published in 2025 That Every Educator Should Read by Forbes contributor Dan Fitzpatrick. The article highlighted how our large-scale review gives teachers a language for explaining why beautifully written AI outputs might reflect very little learning, and why struggle, reflection, and effort still matter.

Forbes, December 30, 2025

Balancing Opportunity and Risk: Artificial Intelligence and Student Well-Being in Higher Education

Iris Delikoura, Pan Hui

A book chapter in the Encyclopedia of Educational Innovation (Springer) examining how LLMs affect student well-being through the lens of Self-Determination Theory (SDT). We explore how AI tools can both enhance and constrain autonomy, competence, and relatedness in higher education, and outline responsibilities for teachers, students, and policymakers.

Encyclopedia of Educational Innovation, Springer, 2026
